Hello!

My name is Isaac; I grew up in central Texas and graduated from the University of Texas at Austin in May 2026. I achieved bachelor's degrees in Computer Science and in Ancient History from UT. As you can tell by the pairing, I was interested in a wide variety of topics during my time in college.

I am blessed to have been a part of so many projects, clubs, research opportunities, and adventures throughout my time in university. I wouldn't trade it for the world.

I am currently working as a research assistant for the Sign Change research group.

Interests

Having been in sports my whole life (whether it be track, basketball, marching band, volleyball, or quidditch) I'm very interested in the intersections of my computer science knowledge with the world of sports. I work on a ton of projects related to that in my free time, which help contextualize the fast-changing and unpredictable world of sports in a more efficient and palatable manner. A few are listed below:

  • NY Giants Pass Protection Analysis: A results-based Python analysis of the Giants offensive line and pass protection ability, using sack rate per down as a relevant statistic.
  • Texas Quadball Performance Analysis: An investigation into the reasons behind UT's loss to UTSA at the Lone Star Cup tournament in 2025; by using tools like ffmpeg and yt-dlp for data processing and Tableau for presentation several core issues were found.
  • IOL Performance in Failed Protection: An analysis of the capabilities of offensive linemen in the NFL to react to incoming sacks and provide the quarterback with a lane to escape further up the pocket or more time to throw. This culminated in a ranking of IOL league-wide based on three new metrics.

Academic Research

  • Sign Change: I've been working as an undergraduate research assistant in the Sign Change research group, implementing a variety of algorithmic solutions to linguistic issues. This has taken the form of ranking sign languages on Levenshtein distance, automatically processing full signs into their component parts and grouping by parameters, and implementing neural networks to cluster different signs into cognate groups.
